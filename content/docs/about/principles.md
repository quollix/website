## Power Should Be Distributed

Digital infrastructure is a political matter. When a small number of large companies control everyday digital services, they control everyday life. Big tech monopolies force people and entire organizations into dependence, while abusing their power to maximize profits.

Power at that scale undermines the foundations of a free society. People should not have to give up autonomy just to participate in digital life.

## Privacy Is a Human Right

Digital infrastructure should protect privacy by default. People should be able to debate and organize without being spied on.

Governments, intelligence services, and companies should all respect that, but often do not. A society that depends on systems that can be watched, restricted, or switched off from above is not free.

## The Commons Must Endure

Software that people depend on should remain a public commons, not become a proprietary trap. Companies may come and go, ownership may change, and business models may evolve, but users should not lose control over the infrastructure they rely on. The goal is to ensure that no single actor can unilaterally take control of the ecosystem and turn a shared foundation into a source of dependency.
